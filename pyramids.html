Pyramids

The Pharoah is dead! Long live the Pharoah! You have just finished working on constructing the great pyramid in which the old Pharoah shall spend eternity, unfortunately the new Pharoah wants you to join him. He has entombed you in the pyramid that you yourself helped build to aid with repairs in his father's afterlife. Using your skills you must escape before Osiris can pass judgement on your souls. And hey, there seems to be a lot of jewels around here nobody is using...

The Pyramid is a mystical place, built to a specification to allow maximum alignment with the stars, and as such you can expect to see and hear things you would never have dreamed were possible.

Playing the Game

This game is an RPG for one to many players and a Pyramid Master (PM). Before starting players generate their characters using the rules below, while the PM generates a pyramid for the players attempt their escape from using the rules overleaf. The objective for the players is to escape the pyramid by using their skills to get through three chambers which contain various challenges.

The PM will describe each chamber and it is up to the players how they will proceed, describing the actions they wish to take to the PM. If any actions are non-trivial, for example jumping a large gap or persuading someone you are in fact an estate agent, the PM will call on a player to make a skill check. Players have three skills with which they can do this:

  • Strength - The physical prowess of the character, used in the moving of heavy objects or the slaying of foes.
  • Intellect - How mentally agile a character is. Useful when disabling traps or studying hieroglyphs.
  • Charm - Is your character silver tongued, or perhaps a fool with a foot in his mouth? Charm is used for persuasion, coercion or even flirtation.
To pass the action a player must roll underneath their skill's value on 2d4, adding the two dice together. A player's skills base values are taken from their profession determined below but can be modified up or down with items which are also detailed later. The PM may decide that an action a player wishes to perform is abnormally difficult, for example backflipping while throwing a dagger or recalling every single Egyptian God in reverse alphabetical order, in this case they may ask the player to succeed twice in a row on a skill check to proceed.

Each player starts play with a single item chosen from the items table. Upon completing the first and second chamber each player will be rewarded with a single item determined by rolling on the items table. Some items will have an affect on stats which should be taken into account when making skill checks. Other items may not have a statistical effect but will allow the player to describe actions that would perhaps otherwise be impossible, for example using a rope to swing across a chasm, it is up to the PM and player to agree on whether an item is useful in any given situation.

When a player makes a skill check and both dice results are the same, i.e. double ones, twos, threes or fours, then the Stars Have Aligned. Pyramids are places of high strangeness and who knows what kind of mystical forces are at play, when the stars have aligned the player must make a d44 roll and check the result on the star alignment table. Alignments can have gameplay effects like stat changes or can introduce a new element role play element to the chamber that the PM will have to describe to players. The PM can ask players to reroll on the alignment table if an effect has already occured once and doesn't make sense to happen again.

Every player starts with 3 health, the PM can inflict damage on you when you fail checks or trigger traps. If you fall to zero health you die and lose the game.

The d44

Some of the roll tables in these rules require rolling a d44. To do so roll a d4 twice; once for the tens and once for the ones. For example Apophis first rolls a three and then a two when gaining an item, this means he should look at the row with 32 as the d44 roll result.

Golden Rule

The golden rule of Pyramids is to have fun and not take it too seriously. Don't be afraid to do silly voices or describe ridiculous situations and actions.

Pyramid Master Guide

It is the Pyramid Master's job to set the scene for the players and keep the game on track. Although you will often find yourself in an adversarial position to players, remember its about everyone having fun and not about winning.

Pyramid Creation

Use the following tables to create the pyramid that the players need to escape from, you can either roll (d6) or choose. Each chamber has a short description that should be used as a jumping off point for your imagination. At the start of each chamber take some time to describe the chamber and answer any questions the players may have about the situation they find themselves in. You will definitely have to improv some answers! A more advanced PM may wish to ignore the tables and create their own adventure, just remember that each chamber should be slightly weirder and harder than the last.

d6 Description
1 The Mercury Room - Many streams of toxic mercury divide up the room into a strange pattern. The fumes are deadly if exposed for long enough.
2 The Room of Reflection - You are met with your doppelgangers, their positions mirror yours so you cannot just walk past.
